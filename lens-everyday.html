Lens: Everyday / Human

“Still functioning” is not the same as “healthy.”

Clinical depletion often looks like coping that never ends: outputs continue while recovery shrinks.

Signs

  • Rest that doesn’t restore
  • More effort for the same outcomes
  • Narrowing tolerance for stress
  • Patchy functioning
  • Recovery takes longer
CD interpretation: load is exceeding recovery often enough that capacity is being used up — even if performance continues.

Not blame

  • Not weakness
  • Not moral failure
  • Not solved by “try harder”
CD shifts attention to conditions: pace, boundaries, support, time for recovery.
CD focuses on capacity, limits, and recovery — not blame, optimisation, or spectacle.
