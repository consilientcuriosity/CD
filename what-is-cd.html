What is Consilient Design

CD is a framework for designing and governing systems so they can stay healthy over time.

Many systems collapse while still meeting targets. CD helps you notice capacity loss early — and redesign before the system becomes brittle.

The problem

Systems can look “fine” while becoming less able to adapt:

  • Outputs continue, but recovery slows.
  • Resilience shrinks, even if performance remains high.
  • Small stresses cause outsized failures later.
CD focuses on what’s being used up.
A system can keep functioning while losing the ability to recover.

What CD does differently

  • Performance → capacity: not just what the system produces, but what it can sustain.
  • Growth → limits: limits are real conditions, not obstacles to overcome.
  • Output → recovery: recovery is part of design, not a personal responsibility.
  • Events → patterns: clinical depletion is gradual and path-dependent.
CD is not a judgement tool.
It does not assign blame. It helps redesign conditions.
Ethics

Care of Living Systems

Life requires recovery. Design must protect regeneration, not just outputs.

Ethics

Care of Human Systems

Culture, dignity, and wellbeing are structural conditions — not rewards for coping.

Ethics

Balance of Distribution and Limits

Fairness includes respecting limits. Distribution without limits becomes extraction.

Plain-language definition

Consilient Design (CD) is a systems framework for designing, observing, and governing complex systems so they remain healthy, fair, and resilient over time.
