Lens: Education & Learning

Learning systems fail when speed replaces care for capacity.

A school can keep producing work while losing depth, curiosity, and capability.

Signs

  • More tasks, less meaning
  • Compliance replaces curiosity
  • Feedback arrives too late
  • Teacher effort rises while growth plateaus
  • Wellbeing becomes compliance
CD interpretation: the system is spending human capacity faster than it regenerates it.

Design moves

  • Slow pace to match recovery
  • Reduce assessment density
  • Build recovery loops (reflection, consolidation)
  • Measure capability growth
  • Protect dignity and cultural respect
CD doesn’t lower standards. It protects the conditions required for genuine learning.
CD focuses on capacity, limits, and recovery — not blame, optimisation, or spectacle.
